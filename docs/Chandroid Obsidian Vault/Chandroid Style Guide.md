>"Make the domestic more industrial, and make the industrial more domestic."
## Props
Do **not:**
- make devices too ergonomic (shaped to the body)
- use grey colors
- use sharp angles

Do Include:
- antennas
- textured lights
- LED lights
- antigravity
- seams
- screw holes
- battery compartments
- yellow, aged/vintage plastic
- CRT screens

Avoid:
- natural materials on manmade objects (foliage is fine)
- unrounded cubic shapes

When designing an electronic appliance with a common design, remix the form while retaining the function. Flip the design upside down, or on its side, or use another appliance's form as inspiration.

When designing an otherwise non-mechanical object, use metallic parts, screws, zippers, locks, hinges, and other stereotypically "mechanical" pieces to inject character.

Favor rounded corners and edges.

## Architecture
- Entrances, holes, and corners almost always have a rounded smooth plastic lip
