## Flow
- property claims are legitimate if based in scarce means
	- *what is the purpose of property?*
		- conflict avoiding norm
			- `Definition of Conflict: situation where two are trying to implement the same means towards incompatible ends`
	- originator of an idea can still use it, therefore no conflict
		- ultimately, it's a question of who gets control over the competitor's property
	- *"You are depriving them of income/accolades/work"*
		- there are many ways to do that which we agree are simply competition
			- therefore, those things are not what determines a just property claim
		- ~~you are entitled to property, not the value of the property (labor is irrelevant)~~
	- computer data is not property (but your hard drive is)
- grants artists undue power over others' property
	- up to and including state violence for non-compliance
	- moral outrage is insufficient grounds to gain property rights
		- you can't stop someone from doing something immoral with their property
	- conflict initiating norm (incentivizes conflict initiation)
- humanity functions on reusing ideas
	- purpose of communication (without this, society could not form)
	- conflict cannot form over the use of non-scarce means
		- reusing ideas is necessarily just
	- *what determines an idea that cannot be justly reused?*
